God will ride in a Volkswagon

I was elected to my first church in 1974. Having answered the call to preach in 1967, I was exited about pastoring my first church. It was out in the country and the children were small and it was the ideal place for them. We had a river not far from the house and we had a place where we could raise a couple of hogs a year.


A few years back before that I had bought a 1965 volkswagon beetle. I was small enough to get in it then. The church being small, I had to work and I drove 72 miles a day to work. I had to leave home at four in the morning to be at work at five when we worked 10 hours. My wife and I had it hard back in those days but I would not trade it for anything. Having to drive from Salem, SC and most of it in pitch black darkness, you have to do something to pass the time.


I learned to play the harmonica, pray, sing, and freeze to death if you did not drive as fast as you could to keep warm. If you did not the heater would not blow out any hot air. I drove through the snow, sleet, rain, and along the way, some sunshine.


I since have had some better and finer cars but none that I thought anymore of than my VW.
